Otonokizaka High School Idols

The girls are popularly known as µ's. They are a group of nine students from Otonokizaka Academy who became school idols to save their school from being shut down. Their purpose was to bring attention to the school. The group disbanded when the third years graduated.

The name µ's comes from Greek mythology's "nine goddesses of music", composed of nine members. Nozomi Tojo was the one who gave the name to the group since she thought it was a fit as they were also composed of nine members.

The nine members of μ's (left to right): Toujou Nozomi, Hoshizora Rin, Koizumi Hanayo, Minami Kotori, Kousaka Honoka, Sonoda Umi, Yazawa Nico, Nishikino Maki, Ayase Eli

Uranohoshi Girls' Academy School Idols

From the sunny shores of Uchiura, Numazu comes Aqours. Like their fellow school idols from Otonokizaka High, they are a group of nine schoolgirls who became idols in order to save Uranohoshi Girls' Academy from shutting down and being merged with another school in Numazu.

Takami Chika, inspired by μ's, gathered her friends and formed the group Aqours. The name could also be pronounced as aqua, with Uchiura being a seaside county. They participated in the Love Live school idol competition to save their school.

Love Live! School Idol Festival

Love Live! School Idol Festival is a social rhythm game for mobile devices (both for iOS and Android) produced by KLabGames and bushiroad. In the game, the player creates units based on collectible virtual cards and performs Live concerts with a designated unit.

All the songs used in the game are sung by members of the Love Live! franchise idol groups: μ's, A-RISE and Aqours.
